# Phase 6: Visualization Synthesis & Plot Generation

This document catalogs the 10 critical data visualizations generated by our PCA pipeline. Each plot serves as a mathematical proof or empirical validation of our quantitative methodology, suitable for inclusion in the final PhD-level research paper.

## 01. Raw Asset Prices (`01_raw_prices.png`)
**Caption:** A time-series representation of the raw, unscaled asset universe. This plot visually demonstrates the extreme scale disparities between asset classes (e.g., S&P 500 index points vs. Treasury Yield percentages), mathematically necessitating the Z-score standardization step to prevent high-variance assets from artificially dominating the principal components.

## 02. Stationary Log Returns (`02_stationary_returns.png`)
**Caption:** The transformed, standardized log-return series across all 10 assets. This visualization confirms that the $I(1)$ price series have been successfully mapped to a stationary $I(0)$ manifold, satisfying the prerequisite mathematical bounds for accurate covariance estimation and subsequent eigendecomposition.

## 03. Cross-Asset Correlation Matrix (`03_correlation_matrix.png`)
**Caption:** A heat map of the Pearson correlation matrix (equivalent to the standardized sample covariance matrix $\Sigma$). This chart reveals the complex linear dependency structure of the global macro environment, highlighting structural inverse relationships (e.g., USD vs. Gold) and beta-driven clusters.

## 04. Scree Plot of Eigenvalues (`04_scree_plot.png`)
**Caption:** A descending bar chart of the extracted eigenvalues $\lambda_k$, representing the variance explained by each orthogonal principal component. The steep decay vividly illustrates the "collapse of dimensionality" in financial markets, where a vast majority of the systemic risk is concentrated within the first few latent factors.

## 05. Cumulative Variance Explained (`05_cumulative_variance.png`)
**Caption:** A step-plot charting the cumulative sum of the variance ratios. This empirically validates our decision to truncate the eigenspace at $k=3$, proving that the first three Principal Components capture sufficient market information (often >60-70%) while filtering out idiosyncratic Marchenko-Pastur noise.

## 06. PC1 Asset Loadings: The Market Risk Factor (`06_loadings_pc1.png`)
**Caption:** A horizontal bar chart of the eigenvector loadings ($\mathbf{v}_1$) for the first principal component. This proves that PC1 acts as a proxy for systemic "Market Risk," characterized by universally high directional loadings during periods of global equity sell-offs and dollar liquidity squeezes.

## 07. PC2 & PC3 Loadings: Liquidity & Inflation (`07_loadings_pc2_pc3.png`)
**Caption:** A comparative grouped bar chart for the second and third principal components. It demonstrates how PCA isolates orthogonal, non-correlated macro factors—specifically mapping PC2 to yield/liquidity dynamics and PC3 to commodity/inflation shocks, independent of standard equity beta.

## 08. Latent Space Scatter: PC1 vs PC2 (`08_scatter_pc1_vs_pc2.png`)
**Caption:** A 2D projection of the daily market states onto the PC1-PC2 plane, color-coded by the active regime. This clustering proves that distinct macroeconomic environments (e.g., Crisis vs. Trend) occupy mathematically separable regions within the latent factor space.

## 09. Rolling PC1 Trajectory (`09_rolling_pc1.png`)
**Caption:** A time-series trajectory of the PC1 score, overlaid with extreme standard deviation bands. Massive spikes in this metric empirically map to historical systemic shocks (e.g., COVID-19 crash, 2022 inflation shock), proving the real-time crisis-detection capabilities of the model.

## 10. Regime Timeline vs S&P 500 (`10_regime_timeline.png`)
**Caption:** A composite timeline overlaying the historical S&P 500 price action against background spans colored by the active PCA-detected regime. This serves as the ultimate visual proof that the spectral engine successfully anticipates and categorizes major market phases (Trend, Range, Crisis) dynamically over time.
